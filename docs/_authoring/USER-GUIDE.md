# Practical content from the dembrane user guide (Info Hub)

Source: the published Notion "Info Hub" the dashboard links to. This is the *practical
substance and tone* our docs must match. Fold these concrete steps and tips into the
relevant pages (host guides especially). Tone target: warm, direct, step-by-step, real tips.

## Setting up a project
- *Name* is dashboard-only (participants never see it).
- *Context*: describe the project "as you would to a sincerely interested, curious friend" -
  goals, the sessions, the questions, anything unique. Include place names, locations,
  common abbreviations.
- *Conversation language*: must match what participants will actually speak. If a Dutch
  conversation is set to English, it gets transcribed as English. Pick the spoken language.
- *Key terms* ("specific contexts", advanced): add names/terms/phrases that matter - e.g.
  without defining "dembrane" here, it gets misspelled in transcripts.
- *Customise the experience*: choose which tutorial participants see, which tags apply, turn
  on experimental features, and set what's shown on the portal while people record.
- *Notifications & reports*: participants can leave an email at the end to be notified when
  reports are ready or updated - great for post-event follow-up.
- *Participant vs admin views*: participants only ever see the portal; the dashboard
  (managing + analysing) is for owners and admins.

## Recording a first conversation (two ways)
- *QR code*: auto-created, shown at the top of the project page. "Open guide" -> QR +
  instruction page you can edit and download as a PDF to print. You can see the number of
  ongoing conversations and open/close the portal for participation. To scan: open phone
  camera, point at code, tap the link -> participant portal.
- *Direct link*: copy and share the portal link by app, email, or browser.
- In the portal: participants can name their session (if enabled), there's a *mic check*,
  press start (a timer runs), pause anytime.
- *Live monitoring*: the conversation shows up in the dashboard once started. The transcript
  appears ~30s-1min after starting. Why the wait? Audio is sent in 30-second pieces; the
  (higher-quality) transcription takes a little extra processing time.

## Ready Check Go (tell participants this before they record)
- Be on good wifi or 5G.
- When prompted, allow microphone access.
- Keep the screen on. A black screen = no recording.
- Tip: a well-charged phone is less likely to sleep.
- Turn on Do Not Disturb - better privacy, fewer interruptions.

## Analysing your data (two ways)
- *Ask (chat) - interactive deep-dive*: click "Ask question", pick conversations (auto-selects
  if you're viewing one), start broad ("What are the main themes?"), then zoom in ("What
  concerns came up about X?"), then ask for evidence ("Show me the quotes"). Best for
  comparing viewpoints, finding quotes, testing a hunch. Templates live in the chat; more in
  the prompt library.
- *Report - automatic synthesis*: click "Report", it analyses all conversations (a few
  minutes), then share the link or download. Best for quickly capturing the atmosphere and
  the main questions, to send to participants (incl. those who couldn't attend). Enable
  participant updates in the portal editor to auto-send to people who left an email.

## Templates (for large-scale, comparable analysis)
- A template = a structure you ask the model to fit the analysis into, so many different
  conversations come out in the same format (easy to compare and report).
- Start small - chat always gives more than you asked; sometimes that's inspiration, often
  you don't need it.
- Use `[ ]` brackets to define what you expect as output (e.g. title, description [max 100
  words], a quote taken directly from the transcript).
- Ask for quotes in the *original language* plus an English translation; count quotes per
  theme without double-counting; keep participants' own wording and tone.
- There's a Dutch PDF manual "Hoe stel ik een template samen" and a prompt library.

## Designing a session
- Common shapes: a plenary part + collecting audience questions + live feedback; and breakout
  sessions. Case-study PDFs (EN/NL) show how dembrane was woven into real events.

## Uploading other documents
