# dembrane — Getting Started (general release)

**Draft v2** · screen-recording walkthrough + voiceover · target runtime **~3:30**

**Why this shape:** loop-first — we open on *what dembrane does*, then show how it's
organised (Act 2, where the new workspaces live). But instead of "everything starts with a
project," Act 1 is framed around dembrane's **two surfaces**: the **portal** (where people
talk and record) and the **dashboard** (where you set up and make sense of it). We open
with **both on screen** so the portal is a co-star from second one — a project is just how
you spin one up.

**Tone:** calm, plain, confident. Short sentences. No hype. "dembrane" (lowercase), never
"ECHO". "Language model", not "AI". British spelling (*organisation*) to match the UI.

**Audience switches:** 🆕 = lands for new users · ♻️ = lands for existing users.

---

## ACT 1 — How dembrane works (the loop) · ~0:00–1:45

### 1. Cold open — the two surfaces 🆕♻️ · ~0:00–0:22
> **VISUAL:** split screen / two windows — **portal** on one side (phone, mic check, live
> transcript), **dashboard** on the other (conversations list). This is the "both screens
> open" you offered to record.

**VO:** "Hi, I'm Sameer from dembrane. dembrane helps you capture a real conversation — a
workshop, a town hall, an interview — and turn hours of talking into something you can
actually use. There are two sides to it. On one screen, the **portal** — that's where
people talk and record. On the other, the **dashboard** — where you set things up, and make
sense of what was said. People speak; dembrane listens, transcribes, and helps you
understand it. Let me walk you through both, start to finish."

### 2. A project, and its portal 🆕 · ~0:22–0:45
> **VISUAL:** dashboard — create-a-project flow (name, context, language) → land on the
> project overview where the **portal link + QR code** are front and centre.

**VO:** "It starts on the dashboard. You create a project — give it a name, a bit of
context, and the language people will actually speak. And every project comes with its own
**portal**: a link, and a QR code. That portal is the front door for everyone taking part."

### 3. People record 🆕 · ~0:45–1:08
> **VISUAL:** phone scans the QR → portal mic check → recording. Cut to dembrane Go app, then
> a quick glimpse of the upload screen.

**VO:** "Share that QR code, or the link, and people record straight from their own phone —
no account, no app to install. Recording in the room yourself? dembrane Go, our iOS app,
captures it right on your phone. And if you already have transcripts, you can upload those
too."

### 4. It transcribes itself 🆕 · ~1:08–1:22
> **VISUAL:** dashboard — a conversation appears; transcript fills in after a beat.

**VO:** "As people talk, dembrane transcribes in the background — securely, in their own
language. The first transcript shows up about a minute in. You don't have to do anything."

### 5. Make sense of it 🆕♻️ · ~1:22–1:45
> **VISUAL:** back on the dashboard — open **Ask**, "What are the main themes?", a follow-up,
> then "Show me the quotes" with citations highlighted → cut to **Report** generating, then a
> shareable report.

**VO:** "Back on the dashboard, the part that matters. Open **Ask**, and question all your
conversations at once — 'What are the main themes?', then 'What did people say about this?',
then 'Show me the quotes.' Every answer points back to who said it. And when you want the
whole picture in one place, generate a **Report** — a shareable summary, ready to send back
to the people who took part."

## ACT 2 — How it's organised (org · workspace · project) · ~1:45–3:12

### 6. The three levels 🆕♻️ · ~1:45–2:08
> **VISUAL:** the new search-first home screen; sidebar — search + inbox up top,
> organisations with workspaces nested inside.

**VO:** "As you do more of this, you'll want it organised. dembrane has three levels. Your
**organisation** — that's your company. Inside it, **workspaces**. And inside those, your
**projects**. The home screen is built around search, so a recent project is always a
keystroke away."

### 7. Workspaces, and who can see them ♻️🆕 · ~2:08–2:38
> **VISUAL:** workspace list — a couple of internal ones, one per client → create-workspace
> visibility options: everyone in the organisation / only people I invite / just me.

**VO:** "A **workspace** is one body of work with its own boundary — its own people, its own
settings. You might keep a couple of internal workspaces, and a separate one for each
client. They stay separate — a client only ever sees their own. When you create one, you
choose who can see it: everyone in your organisation, only the people you invite, or just
you."

### 8. Billing, lightly ♻️ · ~2:38–2:48
> **VISUAL:** brief hover over billing on an internal vs a client workspace.

**VO:** "Your internal workspaces share your organisation's plan, so make as many as you
like. A client's workspace stands on its own."

### 9. Members, roles, and outside collaborators ♻️🆕 · ~2:48–3:12
> **VISUAL:** Members tab → invite modal (email + role dropdown) → send → switch to "Peter's"
> account showing only the one client workspace.

**VO:** "To work together, open **Members**, add a colleague's email, pick a role, and send.
They'll join the same projects and conversations, right alongside you. And you can bring
someone in from outside — a client, say — as an **external collaborator**. They see only the
one workspace you invited them to. Nothing else."

## CLOSE · ~3:12–3:35

### 10. Reassurance + sign-off 🆕♻️
> **VISUAL:** quick montage — portal editor, chat, conversations, report — then back to a
> feedback button highlighted on screen.

**VO:** "If you've used dembrane before — everything you relied on is right where you left
it. The portal, your chats, conversations, reports: all the same. What's new is the
structure around them. So that's dembrane, end to end: open the portal to collect a
conversation, and use the dashboard to make sense of it. If anything feels off, or you've
got an idea, there's a feedback button on every screen. Use it — we read all of it."
